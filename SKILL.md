# The Elements of Style

Apply the principles from William Strunk Jr.'s *The Elements of Style* (1918) to produce clear, vigorous, concise prose.

## Reference

The full principle reference is in `elements-of-style.md` (~4KB). Read it before writing or revising.

## When to Use

Use this skill whenever writing or editing prose meant for human readers:
- Articles, blog posts, essays
- Emails, messages, social media posts
- Marketing and sales copy
- Documentation and reports
- Books and long-form projects
- Any text where clarity and conciseness matter

## How to Apply

### For new writing
1. Read `elements-of-style.md`
2. Write the draft applying the principles throughout
3. Review the draft against Rules 10-13 and 18 (active voice, positive form, concrete language, omit needless words, emphatic endings) — these are the most impactful

### When rules conflict
Rule 12 (specific, concrete language) outranks Rule 13 (omit needless words). Never cut words that add specificity in the name of conciseness. A shorter sentence that says less is not an improvement.

### For revisions
1. Read `elements-of-style.md`
2. Make a pass focused on each of these, in order:
   3. **Sharpen**: Replace vague language with specific, concrete details (Rule 12). This comes first because specificity is the highest priority.
   4. **Cut**: Omit needless words (Rule 13). Remove filler like *the fact that*, *case*, *character*, *nature*. But do not cut words that carry specific meaning.
   5. **Activate**: Convert passive to active voice (Rule 10). Convert negative to positive form (Rule 11).
   6. **Structure**: Check paragraph unity (Rule 8), topic sentences (Rule 9), parallel form (Rule 15), emphasis at end (Rule 18).
   7. **Polish**: Fix comma splices (Rule 5), dangling participles (Rule 7), misused words (Section IV). Remove em dashes wherever possible — one, carefully deployed, per piece is all you need.

### When context is limited
Dispatch a subagent with the draft text and `elements-of-style.md`. Have the subagent revise and return the result.
